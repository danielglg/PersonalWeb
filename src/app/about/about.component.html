How did I arrive here?

Curiosity, creativity and socializing have always driven my growth.

When I was a child I enjoyed reading, painting, playing sports and building amazing models with creative toys such as Lego and K'nex. Some of my works won competitions at school and high-school, being probably the most impressive the hat contest where I presented a hat attachable to my head which had motors powered with small solar panels.

Later on, composing music became my main passion. I learned the basics from a mentor, the rest came with self-study and practice. It meant too the inception of me as a teacher, firstly as collaborator of my mentor and afterwards on my own. At some point I stopped teaching music and started teaching maths, physics and languages.

As a consequence of realizing over the course of these years that I wanted to design, build and produce things, I opted for a technical path. Next, I focused towards the engineering world and eventually decided to pursue my university studies in telecommunication engineering (with strong specialization on those areas I liked the most). My enthusiasm transformed into grit, positively impacting the outcome of my efforts; for instance, I successfully took more courses than the required on the degree and my end-of-degree project was awarded by a top company.

Once accomplished such milestone I honestly faced a very challenging context at my arrival to the job market. Nonetheless I was ready, available and well-prepared (not just due to the knowledge acquired but also due to my work ethic and self-learning skill), so I persisted and eventually fixed the setback.

Events of different kind presented me opportunities; chances to: meet new amazing teammates, learn new tools and ways of working, discover new sectors, resolve new challenges, interact with new perspectives, travel, ...

As a result, in retrospect, I can say that I am currently as such engineer focused on designing, developing and deploying innovative systems and services, but enriched and more flexible. And in addition, I added travelling and languages to the set of main activities I enjoy.
