The problem: context & objectives

Context:

The Ultra-HD or UHDTV (Ultra High Definition Television) emerged as the natural evolution of the HD (High Definition) and 3DTV formats, aiming a more realistic experience for the end-user in an immersive environment, thanks to their enhanced viewing conditions. The UHDTV standard defines two formats named 4K and 8K, with four and sixteen times the HD resolution respectively. However, UHDTV is much more than higher spatial resolution, it involves a higher temporal resolution, an enhanced colorimetry, a higher bit depth, and a higher dynamic range than the previous formats.

These improvements imply data increase, a storage increase, faster real-time professional interfaces above 10Gbps, and bigger display sizes with which to enjoy high fidelity content. To overcome the challenges the whole broadcast industry and the administration have invested in bringing such technology to market. UltraHD-4U, a research project of the European Union in the scope of its Eureka program (CATRENE office), was borned to coordinate such efforts along the value-chain.

The project goal was to set up a successful end-to-end 4K Ultra HDTV chain (broadband and/or broadcast) for use at consumer premises and/or at dedicated show-points. As far as I remember, the project consortium was composed by the following partners at that time : Alioscopy, Arçelik, Barco, Binocle, Hispasat, mediAVentures, Pace, SAPEC, STM, Technicolor, Thomson Video Networks, University of Nantes and Vitec. Most of these enterprises are contributors to standardization bodies like DVB, SMPTE and MPEG.

Objectives:
  • Fostering the adoption of Ultra-HD in Europe through technical research performed within UltraHD-4U project.
  • Contributing to Work Package 2 of the project, which studies the content delivery architecture from the acquisition to the distribution (addressing aspects of content acquisition, preparation, content encoding and distribution).
  • Studying the new H.265 (HEVC) video compression standard and capitalizing such knowledge among teammates of Sapec and Technical University of Madrid.
  • Helping Sapec to create the software and media files required to achieve the previous goals, in order to transfer innovation to its professional encoders and decoders.

Approach (tools and knowledge):
  • Modern video and audio coding standards (see in keywords).
  • C++ application that based on the parameters of the command line automates the creation fo the Transport Stream (TS) multiplex.

    Media formats supported are:

    • Video:
      • H.265
      • H.264
    • Audio:
      • AC-3
      • Enhanced AC-3
      • MPEG 1 Layer I, II, and III.
      • MPEG 2 Layer I, II, and II

    Firstly, legacy software was understood. Later, this legacy software was efficiently encapsulated in a newer and clearer software architecture that I designed (more suited for the offline nature of the application). To this architecture, each feature (e.g. new coding format) was developed, integrated and tested iteratively.

    Among the elements of this software architecture there are several threads:

    1. stream managers (modules that use specific stream analyzers and circular buffers to keep efficiently current interesting data),
    2. reporting module (it filters and manages how messages are displayed to the user or logged according to verbose and debugging levels),
    3. multiplexer thread.

    For managing concurrency, there are synchronization variables and mechanisms distributed along the previous threads, etc.

    The application was coded following high quality coding standards and documentation was generated using Doxygen. Moreover, the application was optimized when and where optimization was suitable for the business needs (for example, adjust the size of circular buffers to suitable sizes according to UltraHD screen resolutions and bitstream structure).

  • Main development tools: Microsoft Visual Studio (C++/C/C#; non-managed classes were preferred in the application), SourceSafe, Doxygen, Notepad++ and Modelio.
  • Perl & batch scripts to automate video quality measures based on VideoClarity output reports.

    VideoClarity products produce structured text documents (logs and other results) that are parsed to generate awesome spreadsheets, containing only the interesting information (e.g. date, files; PSNR, DMOS, JND quality measures) and presenting auto-generated graphics.

  • Scripts using batch files, FFmpeg, and HEVC encoder.
  • Media stream analyzer from Sencore, Elecard and MainConcept.
  • Log files of Sapec’s encoders and decoders

  • Trained colleagues following the previous course.

    2. flaws existing in analyzers of Elecard and MainConcept.
