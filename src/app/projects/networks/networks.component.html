Networks

All my projects have involved telecommunication networks and this section is specifically focused on mobile networks. Next I provide a very brief summary of key concepts needed to understand the projects where I worked. The scope is kept to basically 4G and 5G technologies.

Mobile network architecture

2G/3G network architectures already split the architecture in two main parts: the Core Network (CN) and the Radio Access Network (RAN). Both are connected together through the backhaul, the RAN is the infrastructure with the base stations communicating signals through air and the CN connects to voice and data networks. With 4G, data neworks become predominant and the ensemble of CN, RAN and backhaul evolves, acquiring the name of Evolved Packet System (EPS). Most of the components and subcomponents of the system are renamed as well, for instance CN becomes Evolved Packet Core (EPC) and RAN Radio Interface.

The architecture experiments a paradigm change during the evolution of 4G: Control and User Plane Separation (CUPS). This change contributes to the development of network improvements and technologies like Software Defined Networks (SDN) and Network Function Virtualization (NFV). It splits the user function from the control functions, so the parts of the system form part of such planes depending on the function they carry out.

CUPS is kept in 5G, but with 5G components evolve again and with that their names: 5G Core (5GC), Next-Generation RAN (NG-RAN).

At the core the protocols used to link the parts have had an evolution too with these standards. RADIUS and DIAMETER (evolution of RADIUS) are the most important.

The radio network evolution depends more on breakthroughs based on physical improvements in radio transmission, signal processing and other optimizations. It can be consulted on the corresponding standards: WCDMA/HSPA for 3G, Long Term Evolution (LTE) for 4G and New Radio (NR) for 5G.

Scope of my projects

Among all the product offering of Ericsson for the core network, I have contributed only to SAPC. SAPC (Service-Aware Policy Controller) is the PCRF (4G) and PCF (5G) of Ericsson.

The way of working is with work packages distributed across Scrum teams. The work packages were I participated are:

  • Diameter interface Rx - RxRequest AVP.
