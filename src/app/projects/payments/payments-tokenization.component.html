The problem: context & objectives

Tokenization, when applied to data security, is the process of substituting a sensitive data element with a non-sensitive equivalent, referred to as a token, that has no intrinsic or exploitable meaning or value.

Tokenization has become then a technology to secure payments data, becoming a key element in the payment ecosystem specially because it is demanded nowadays by several security norms (e.g. PCI DSS, rules from Royal Bank of India, ...).

On one hand, this has led to the development of specialized tokenization solutions in-house. On the other hand, this has fostered the integrations with multiple providers of token-related services, posing new challenges to clients who have to implement tokenization.

In particular, using tokens rather than payment cards involve a set of operations: tokenization, detokenization, retokenization, token vault management, token lifecycle management, retrieval of associated security data, etc.

Diverse applications and solutions have emerged given the different contexts in which the technology is applied. Some of those where I have contributed have their focus on:

  • Protecting the data communicated, processed and stored by internal systems.
  • Providing interception and vault capabilities, plus the previous, to external systems.
  • Ensuring PCI DSS compliance of customer flows (e.g. HTTP messages, files -batch processes-).
  • Facilitating the integrations of customers with tokenization solutions (internal or external) and in a flexible way.
  • Migrating tokens of a type to tokens of a different type.
