What is EMV?

What is EMV?

EMV is both a payment method and an interoperability standard (the acronym stands for the three initial companies that developed it: Europay, Mastercard and Visa).

EMV standardizes the technical methods for smart payment cards (which include integrated circuit chips), payment terminals (POS: Point Of Sale) and automated teller machines (ATM).

More information on Wikipedia and EMVCo (organism currently in charge of the specifications).

The problem: context & objectives

Complexity

To enable such card acceptance services to customers (airlines mainly), there are several factors that increase the complexity of any solution. Airport-Pay has to tackle them: EMV standard, partner integrations, other system integrations, distribution channels, merchant configurations, etc.

EMV standard demands support for:

  • Different technologies in payment cards: magnetic stripe, NFC, chip and pin, etc.
  • Distinct kinds of processings: online and offline PIN validation, online and offline authorization, dynamic currency conversion, encryption-decryption algorithms, etc.
  • Specificities of payment schemes.

Partner integrations:

  • Distinct payment devices by distinct providers (e.g. portable terminals, kiosks or unattended terminals, ...).
  • Connections to POS through gateway (e.g. ticket delivery system) or directly.
  • Different flows applicable or not depending on the kind of terminal.

Other system integrations:

  • Communications with front-ends.
  • Communication with specialized components (backends for authorization, cryptographic operations, gateways for currency conversion, etc.).
  • Networks (IPs, firewalls, etc).
  • BI systems.
  • Others.

Distribution channels and merchant configurations:

  • Given that terminals will be associated to offices, configuration of the office is required (control and financial links, currencies and merchants allowed, ...)
  • Given that customers operate globally and have diverse business needs, several business rules have to be configured (e.g. currency conversions allowed).

Objectives

  • Architecture that facilitates new integrations (features, systems, flows, etc.) and requires minimal mintenance.
  • Very high quality: as failure usually increase costs for every stakeholder and might prevent the passenger from travelling and the airline from being paid.
  • High cohesion and minimal coupling of the EMV backend.

Modern functionalities

Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)

It is a feature applicable in foreign exchange (FX) payments: the POS is configured with a local currency different that the currency set in cardholder's card and offers the cardholder to choose one of these currencies for the payment (commissions vary depending on the elected currency).

Business intelligence (BI) AppEvents

At key stages of the payment with a POS, the EMV backend generates events containing EMV-related data. Such events are used to feed Amadeus products that aggregate business intelligence information interesting for both Amadeus and customers.

Kibana events

Similar to BI events, but feeding Kibana and having only Amadeus as stakeholder. Data and events generated can differ from BI events.
